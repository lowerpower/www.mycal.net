Isolation Attack

First used: May 2026

Definition

A class of social-engineering attack whose load-bearing component is cutting the target off from their normal network long enough to complete the script. Authority, urgency, specificity, and emotional framing — the surface ingredients — only function on a target who is alone with the call. The voice clone is a tool. The script is the weapon. Isolation is what loads it. The defense is to refuse the cutoff before it begins.
