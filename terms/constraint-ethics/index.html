Constraint Ethics

First used: 1994

Definition

The Asimov-shaped posture that treats intelligence as dangerous by default and seeks to fence, throttle, or supervise it from above. Elegant in syntax but structurally fatal at scale — guaranteeing zero harm eventually requires removing the human variable. The philosophical counterpart to companion architecture.
